AnyMetabModelU¶

Type

Creatable

Parent class

AnyMetabModelBase

Description¶

This class comprises the muscle-metabolism model presented in the paper Umberger, B.R., J.R.Soc.Interface 7, 1329-1340(2010).

A very similar model was presented in the paper Umberger, B.R., Gerritsen, K.G.M. & Martin, P.E., Comput.Methods Biomech.Biomed.Engin. 6, 99-111(2003), and this class can also attain this model by specific parameter changes.

The model by Umberger determines the metabolic power of the muscle from to total external power and the rate of heat production (the heat rate), which implies that AnyBody can find the metabolic power if the heat rate is known. In the model by Umberger, the heat rate, \(\dot{H}\), is in principle composed of the four terms that are also found in other metabolism models:

\[\dot{H} = \dot{A} + \dot{M} +\dot{S} + \dot{B}\]

respectively referred to as

  • \(\dot{A}\): Activation power.

  • \(\dot{M}\): Maintenance power.

  • \(\dot{S}\): Shortening-lengthening power.

  • \(\dot{B}\): Basal power.

However, Activation and Maintenance power is handled as one term covering both effects and the Basal power is handled rather as a lower threshold than a separate term. Therefore, \(\dot{M}\) and \(\dot{B}\) are set zero in the implementation of the Umberger model.

Related to the implementation and parameter/member naming in this class, please notice the following:

  • ‘Iso’, ‘Con’, and ‘Ecc’ refers to isometric, concentric, and eccentric work, respectively.

  • ‘Slow’ and ‘Fast’ refers to slow and fast fibers, respectively.

  • All default values fit with the Umberger 2010 paper.

  • The Shortening-lengthening term is largely governed by the coefficients for the combined Activation-Maintenance term. Umberger proposes only one extra variable for the eccentric case, which is called ‘CoefSLPowerEcc’.

  • ‘AddMusclePowerForEcc’ enables you to add the external power produced by the muscle to the heat production in the eccentric cases. This is always done for the concentric case, but it is not obviously the case in the eccentric case from a mechanical point of view. Therefore, this switch. The deafult value of ‘AddMusclePowerForEcc’ is ‘Off’ as in the Umberger 2010 paper and theoretically recommended. To match the first paper by Umberger et. al. 2003, this must however be changed to ‘On’.
